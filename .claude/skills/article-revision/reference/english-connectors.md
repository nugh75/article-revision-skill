# English connectors for academic writing

Reference list of discourse connectors for academic English, organised by logical function. Use to ensure textual cohesion and clear argument progression.

---

## Preliminary note: zero anaphora

Unlike Italian, **English does not routinely drop the subject**. Zero anaphora (ellipsis of the subject) is ungrammatical in English declarative clauses except in imperative or coordinate structures with identical subjects:

> The framework organises the educational experience into six dimensions. *(subject: the framework)*
> It articulates Practice patterns, Readiness beliefs… *(subject must be explicit)*

In English, use a pronoun (*it, they, this, these*) or a lexical repetition to maintain the topic. The absence of a subject is interpreted as a grammatical error, not a cohesive device.

### Exceptions
- Coordinated VPs with the same subject: *The model identifies patterns **and** articulates dimensions.*
- Imperatives: *Consider the following example.*
- Some fixed expressions: *As shown below, As noted earlier.*

These connectors can be used to ensure cohesion between sentences and paragraphs, to respect theme/rheme progression (given → new), and to make the argumentative logic explicit. In academic writing, every shift between sentences should be signalled by an appropriate connector or a lexical repetition of the theme.

When writing for an Italian academic context, prefer connectors that match the formal register expected by the journal. Avoid overly conversational connectors (*so*, *well*, *you see*).
